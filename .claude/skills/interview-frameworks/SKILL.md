# Interview Prep Skill

## Interview Types & How to Prepare

### Behavioral Interviews (STAR Method)
"Tell me about a time when..."
- Prepare 8–10 STAR stories that can be adapted to multiple questions
- Each story should be 90–120 seconds when spoken
- Practice out loud, not just mentally

### Case Interviews (Consulting/Strategy roles)
- Structure first: state your framework before calculating
- Practice with "Case in Point" or "Case Interview Secrets"
- Show your thinking process, not just the answer

### Technical Interviews (Engineering/Data roles)
- Understand the role's tech stack specifically
- Practice problems on LeetCode, HackerRank, or Codesignal
- For data roles: SQL, Python basics, stats fundamentals

### Portfolio/Work Sample Interviews
- Prepare 3–5 work examples that demonstrate core competencies
- Be ready to narrate the WHY behind decisions, not just the WHAT
- Have metrics for each piece of work

## Common Behavioral Questions by Category

### Leadership
1. "Tell me about a time you led a team through a difficult situation."
2. "Describe a time you had to influence others without formal authority."
3. "Tell me about a time you had to give difficult feedback."
4. "Describe how you've built or scaled a team."

### Problem-Solving
1. "Tell me about the most complex problem you've solved."
2. "Describe a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information."
3. "Tell me about a time a project went off the rails. What did you do?"

### Conflict & Communication
1. "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager. How did you handle it?"
2. "Describe a time you had to manage a difficult stakeholder."
3. "Tell me about a time you had to change someone's mind."

### Achievement & Initiative
1. "What's your proudest professional accomplishment?"
2. "Tell me about a time you went above and beyond your role."
3. "Describe a time you identified and solved a problem no one asked you to solve."

### Failure & Learning
1. "Tell me about a failure and what you learned from it."
2. "Describe a time you received critical feedback. How did you respond?"
3. "What's something you'd do differently in your career?"

### Adaptability
1. "Tell me about a time you had to adapt to a major change."
2. "Describe a time you had to learn something completely new quickly."
3. "How have you handled ambiguity or unclear direction?"

## Common Opening Questions

### "Tell me about yourself"
**Formula** (60–90 seconds):
1. Current role / identity (1 sentence)
2. Career narrative arc (2–3 sentences — connect the dots)
3. Why you're here / what you're excited about (1–2 sentences)

**Coach clients to**: End with something forward-looking, not backward-looking.

### "Why do you want to work here?"
Must be specific. Three components:
1. Something about the company (mission, product, growth stage, team)
2. Something about the role specifically
3. How it connects to where you're headed

**Red flags to avoid**: "Great opportunity for growth", "Exciting company", "Good culture" — all too generic.

### "What are your weaknesses?"
**Formula**:
1. Real weakness (not a "strength in disguise")
2. Self-awareness about why it's a challenge
3. Specific steps you're taking to improve
4. Evidence it's getting better

Example: "I've historically struggled with delegating when under pressure — I default to doing things myself to ensure quality. I've been working on this by proactively identifying tasks to hand off each week and building stronger feedback loops with my team. I've gotten better — in my last role, I went from managing everything myself to successfully delegating 70% of routine work."

## Questions to Always Have Ready

Prepare at least 3, ask 2–3 per interview:

**About the role**:
- "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
- "What are the hardest parts of this role?"
- "What would the ideal candidate look like a year from now?"

**About the team**:
- "How would you describe the team culture?"
- "How does this team handle disagreements or competing priorities?"

**About the company**:
- "What are the biggest challenges the company is facing right now?"
- "What's the most exciting thing on the roadmap?"

**About the interviewer**:
- "What's been your favorite part of working here?"
- "How has your role evolved since you joined?"

## Post-Interview Protocol

Within 24 hours:
1. Send thank-you email to each interviewer separately (not one mass email)
2. Reference something specific from the conversation
3. Reiterate enthusiasm and fit in 2–3 sentences
4. Follow up on the timeline they gave

If you don't hear back by their stated date:
- One polite follow-up email: "I wanted to check in on the status of the [role] — I remain very interested and look forward to hearing from you."
- Wait 1 week, then one final follow-up before moving on
