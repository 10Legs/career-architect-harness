# Resume Patterns Skill

## Bullet Point Writing Patterns

### The STAR-CAR Formula (Primary)
**S**ituation + **T**ask + **A**ction + **R**esult
or
**C**ontext + **A**ction + **R**esult (more concise)

```
[Action Verb] + [What You Did] + [Scale/Context] + [Measurable Result]
```

**Examples**:

BAD: "Responsible for managing the social media team"
GOOD: "Led 4-person social media team to grow combined follower base 67% YoY, generating 23% of total inbound leads"

BAD: "Helped improve customer satisfaction"
GOOD: "Redesigned customer onboarding flow based on NPS analysis, lifting CSAT scores from 72 to 91 over two quarters"

BAD: "Worked on cost reduction initiatives"
GOOD: "Identified $1.4M in annual savings by auditing vendor contracts and renegotiating 12 supplier agreements"

### Quantification Strategies
When you don't have exact numbers, use these legitimate approaches:

| Situation | Pattern |
|-----------|---------|
| Don't know exact % | "significantly increased" → get closer, ask the user |
| Process improvement | "Reduced [X] from [Y] to [Z]" |
| Team size | "Led cross-functional team of [N]" |
| Budget | "Managed $[X]K/M budget" |
| Volume | "Processed/handled/served [N] [customers/requests/cases]" |
| Revenue | "Supported $[X]M revenue organization" |
| Time savings | "Cut [process] time by [N] hours/days per [week/month]" |
| Scale | "Across [N] locations/products/markets/countries" |

### Action Verb Categories

**Leadership**: Led, Directed, Managed, Supervised, Mentored, Coached, Championed, Spearheaded, Established

**Achievement**: Achieved, Exceeded, Surpassed, Delivered, Completed, Attained, Earned

**Analysis**: Analyzed, Evaluated, Assessed, Investigated, Audited, Diagnosed, Researched

**Creation**: Built, Developed, Designed, Created, Launched, Founded, Established, Implemented

**Improvement**: Optimized, Streamlined, Improved, Enhanced, Transformed, Modernized, Revamped

**Growth**: Grew, Scaled, Expanded, Increased, Accelerated, Boosted, Drove

**Savings**: Reduced, Cut, Decreased, Eliminated, Consolidated, Renegotiated

**Collaboration**: Partnered, Collaborated, Coordinated, Facilitated, Aligned, Liaised

## Professional Summary Patterns

### Pattern 1: Direct (Best for clear career continuity)
```
[Title] with [X] years of experience in [specialty]. Expert at [core competency 1] and [core competency 2]. [Differentiating accomplishment or trait]. [Value statement for this role].
```

### Pattern 2: Problem-Solver (Best for roles needing strategic thinking)
```
[Title] who helps [type of organization] [solve specific problem]. Over [X] years, [specific achievement that proves problem-solving]. Known for [distinctive approach or trait]. Seeking to bring [specific value] to [type of opportunity].
```

### Pattern 3: Career Pivot (Best when changing fields)
```
[Previous Title] with [X] years in [previous field] transitioning to [target field]. Deep expertise in [transferable skill 1] and [transferable skill 2] uniquely positions [the user] to [specific value for new field]. [Connecting accomplishment that bridges old and new].
```

## Section Templates by Situation

### Early Career (0–3 years)
Order: Summary → Education → Skills → Experience → Projects
- Lead with education if it's the credential
- Projects section is critical — personal, class, or freelance work counts
- Internships treated as full experience with accomplishment bullets

### Mid-Career (3–10 years)
Order: Summary → Core Competencies → Experience → Education
- Experience is the star — Education moves down
- Summary should lead with title and top differentiator
- Quantify everything

### Career Pivot
Order: Summary → Transferable Skills → Experience → Education
- Summary must explicitly bridge old and new
- Skills section highlights crossover competencies prominently
- Reframe old job bullets to speak to new industry's needs

### Executive (Director+)
Order: Summary → Core Competencies → Professional Experience → Board/Advisory → Education
- Summary is more narrative — 5–6 lines acceptable
- Bullets focus on P&L, org scale, strategic outcomes
- Drop bullet count on early career roles (title + company only if >15 years ago)

## Resume Length by Experience Level

| Experience | Target | Max |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| 0–3 years | 1 page | 1 page |
| 3–10 years | 1 page | 1.5 pages |
| 10–20 years | 1.5–2 pages | 2 pages |
| 20+ / Executive | 2 pages | 2 pages |

**Exceptions**: Academia, research, government, federal contracting (use CV format, no length limit)

## Common Rewrites Reference

### "Responsible for X" → Lead with action
"Responsible for training new employees" → "Onboarded and trained 15+ new hires per quarter, reducing time-to-productivity by 30%"

### Vague adjectives → Evidence
"Excellent communication skills" → "Presented quarterly business reviews to C-suite, influencing $2M+ in budget allocations"

### Team contributor → Impact
"Was part of the team that launched new website" → "Contributed to website redesign that reduced bounce rate 28% and increased conversions 15%"

### Duties list → Accomplishments
"Answered customer calls" → "Resolved 60+ daily customer inquiries while maintaining 97% CSAT rating in high-volume call center"
