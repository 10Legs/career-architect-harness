# Skill Translation Skill

## The Translation Principle

Most people dramatically underestimate the market value of their experience because they describe *what they did* rather than *the capability that doing it proves*. Skill translation bridges that gap.

> Raw Experience + Market Context = Marketable Asset

## The Translation Framework

For each raw experience, apply this formula:

1. **What was the raw task?** (describe literally)
2. **What skill did executing it require?** (extract the capability)
3. **What was the scale/impact?** (quantify or contextualize)
4. **What's the market term for that skill?** (business language)
5. **Can we find an accomplishment here?** (outcome-oriented framing)

### Example: Stay-at-Home Parent Returning to Work

Raw: "Managed household and children for 5 years"

Translation:
- Budget management: "Managed $85K annual household budget, consistently delivering 8–12% under budget through strategic planning and vendor negotiation"
- Project management: "Coordinated care schedules, medical appointments, and developmental programs for 3 children across multiple service providers simultaneously"
- Crisis management: "Navigated multiple time-sensitive health emergencies, demonstrating composure and rapid resource coordination under pressure"
- Procurement: "Researched and selected service providers (pediatricians, tutors, contractors) evaluating quality, cost, and reliability criteria"

## Common Latent Skill Sources to Probe

**Hobbies**:
- Competitive anything → performance under pressure, strategic thinking
- Creative pursuits (music, writing, art) → design thinking, creative problem-solving, attention to detail
- Technical hobbies (woodworking, electronics, cooking) → precision, process optimization
- Physical endurance sports → goal-setting, resilience, self-discipline

**Volunteer Work**:
- Leadership roles → direct management experience
- Fundraising → sales, persuasion, relationship building
- Advocacy → public speaking, policy knowledge, stakeholder engagement
- Teaching/tutoring → training, communication, curriculum design

**Side Projects / Freelance**:
- Client management, scope definition, delivery → consulting skills
- Self-promotion → marketing, branding, business development
- Tool selection, process design → operations, project management

## Phrasing Guide: Reframing Unconventional Experience

| If the user says... | Coach them to say... |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| "I was just a..." | "As a [title], I..." |
| "It was nothing special" | Ask: "What was the outcome? Who benefited?" |
| "Everyone does that" | "Most people don't translate that into data/results" |
| "It was a long time ago" | "Is the skill still current? Then it's still valid" |
| "I don't have real experience in X" | "Tell me about a time you had to [adjacent skill]..." |

## Ethics Guardrail

Translation must be truthful. The goal is accurate representation, not inflation:
- Do NOT invent accomplishments
- Do NOT claim expertise not demonstrated
- Do NOT translate hobbies into professional credentials they aren't
- DO accurately represent the depth and recency of the skill
- DO coach the user to be honest about skill level (beginner vs. proficient vs. expert)
